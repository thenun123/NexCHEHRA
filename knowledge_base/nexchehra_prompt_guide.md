# NexCHEHRA Prompt Engineering Guide

## Introduction
Getting the perfect AI influencer requires writing a good prompt. NexBrain and NexVision rely on how detailed and structured your input is. Use this guide to improve your results.

## Formula for the Perfect Visual Prompt (NexVision)
When describing how your influencer should look, try to follow this structure:
**[Subject Details] + [Setting/Background] + [Lighting] + [Camera/Style settings]**

### Examples:
- **Bad Prompt:** "A girl in a cafe."
- **Good Prompt:** "A 25-year-old Asian female influencer, wearing a casual white oversized hoodie. She is sitting in a modern, brightly lit coffee shop in Tokyo. Sunlight streaming through the window, shallow depth of field, 8k resolution, photorealistic."

## Essential Keywords for High-Quality Influencers
To prevent your generated persona from looking cartoonish or fake, always include these types of "enhancer" keywords:
- **Realism:** "Photorealistic", "cinematic lighting", "high texture details", "raw photo", "DSLR".
- **Facial Features:** Specify eye color, hair texture (e.g., "messy curly dark hair"), and facial expression (e.g., "subtle smirk", "looking directly at the camera").

## Scripting for Voice and Motion (NexBrain / NexMotion)
When you write the script that your influencer will speak, remember:
1. **Pacing:** Use punctuation heavily! Commas (,) and periods (.) force the NexMotion engine to insert natural pauses into the voice and lip-sync.
2. **Emotion:** If your custom voice supports emotion tags, try to match the emotion of the text (e.g., "Hey guys! [Excited] Today I have something amazing to show you!").

## Troubleshooting Bad Generations
- **Face looks distorted?** Ensure you added terms like "perfect facial symmetry" and are not asking the AI to generate full-body shots from too far away.
- **Voice sounds robotic?** Break long sentences up into shorter ones. Add commas.
